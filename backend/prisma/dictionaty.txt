1. Fixed-Term Lease
Definition: A lease with a specific start and end date (e.g., August 1, 2025 – July 31, 2026).

Key Details:

Rent amount is usually locked.

Early termination may result in penalties.

✅ Best for: predictable occupancy, long-term tenants.

🔧 You already support this with startDate and endDate.



2. Month-to-Month Lease (Rolling Lease)
Definition: Automatically renews each month until either party gives notice (often 30 days).

How it works:

Usually begins after a fixed-term lease ends, unless explicitly set from the start.

✅ Best for: short-term flexibility.

💡 Could be handled with:

A isRolling: Boolean or termType: ROLLING | FIXED






🧾 3. Short-Term Lease
Definition: Lease shorter than 6 months.

Examples: Weekly, monthly rentals.

✅ Best for: vacation units, temporary housing.

You already support this using your RentInterval + short dates.



🔐 4. Long-Term Lease
Definition: Lease of 1 year or longer.

✅ Best for: stability, especially in residential areas.

Already covered with your current schema.



📆 5. Lease with Renewal Option
Definition: Tenant has the option to renew the lease before it ends.

Often includes:

Renewal deadline (e.g., “Notify us 60 days before end”)

New rent terms in advance
